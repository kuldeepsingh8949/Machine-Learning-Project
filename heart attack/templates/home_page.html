About the Project :

This project leverages machine learning to predict the likelihood of heart attacks based on patient data such as age, cholesterol level, blood pressure, and more.

In our heart attack prediction project, Logistic Regression plays a critical role in classifying the likelihood of a patient having a heart attack based on various health parameters. This algorithm is well-suited for the task because it helps model the relationship between the input features (such as age, cholesterol levels, blood pressure, etc.) and the binary outcome (heart attack: yes or no).

Key Features for Prediction:

Age: Increased age is a significant risk factor for heart disease.

Sex: Men are generally at higher risk than women for heart attacks.

Cholesterol Levels: High cholesterol can lead to plaque build-up, increasing heart attack risk.

Blood Pressure: Elevated blood pressure is a known factor in heart disease.

Chest Pain Type: Different types of chest pain provide valuable clues about heart health.

Resting Electrocardiographic Results: Provides electrical signals of the heart's activity.

The goal of this machine learning project is to develop a system that predicts the risk of heart disease based on various health indicators. This system will assist healthcare professionals by providing predictions that can be used as part of a diagnostic process.

Dataset Overview :

The dataset used in this project includes medical records of patients with features such as age, sex, cholesterol levels, resting blood pressure, and more. These features are used to train the machine learning model.
